Nature built the first bridges, such as Rainbow Bridge in Utah's Natural Bridges National Monument. Man-made bridges began as tree trunks laid in girder fashion.

The bridging of barriers is important to the story of human settlement. A 16th-century architect and engineer commented that "bridges should befit the spirit of community by exhibiting commodiousness, firmness and delight."
